Founder & CEO

Chad R. Johnston

KCS Capital & 4orm Finance

Systems builder with roots in Canada's oil and gas sector, where he led 100-plus person teams on multi-billion-dollar projects in high-risk, mission-critical environments. After years studying monetary history and capital markets, Chad founded KCS Capital to design the regulated digital infrastructure Canadian finance needs.

His entrepreneurial roots run deep. His grandparents founded the first glass company in Fort McMurray in 1972, securing early contracts with what would become Syncrude and Suncor during the early oil sands expansion. That legacy of building in emerging industries instilled in him a respect for long-term thinking and conviction during uncertain cycles.

Inspired by that example, Chad began his own entrepreneurial path early, launching his first lawn care business at 13. A modest start, but a defining moment. It was the first time he experienced the direct relationship between effort, production, and return. That early lesson in ownership, accountability, and self-reliance shaped the mindset he carries into every venture today.

Chad began his career in Canada's oil and gas and industrial sectors, working his way up through the ranks across a variety of industries, leading large-scale projects involving multi-million-dollar equipment and sizeable field teams in high-risk, mission-critical environments. He played a senior leadership role in the final phase of the multi-billion-dollar CNRL Horizon Coker Upgrade, with care and control of more than 100 workers. For a portion of his career he served as a certified High Angle Rope Rescue Technician, responsible for life-critical decisions under pressure. These experiences shaped his disciplined, risk-aware leadership style.

In 2013, he transitioned into entrepreneurship, launching a specialized mobile oil services company and engineering a custom oil regeneration system from the ground up. The commodity downturn of 2015 reinforced a defining principle: time in the market beats timing the market. He later expanded into multi-industry service businesses, working with startups and focusing on scalable sales models, growth systems, and client retention. Building teams over the years taught him an important lesson: you may not know every answer, but if you build the right team, you will always find them.

Prior to KCS Capital, Chad consulted as an Innovation Lead at IEP Energy Economics Development Corp., where he helped restructure the sales department, refine team alignment, and formalize sales strategy and presentation frameworks. He also contributed to stakeholder engagement initiatives focused on public education around renewable energy investment. His work expanded into the provincial utility and First Nations sectors, where he supported engagement with Indigenous communities and presented alongside provincial leaders. Chad has spoken across Western Canada on renewable infrastructure and investment, most recently as a keynote presenter at the Clean Energy BC Summit.

Driven by a sense of responsibility for the next generation, Chad dedicated several years to studying monetary history, inflation cycles, debt expansion, and modern capital markets. Through this work he identified that financial infrastructure, particularly settlement speed, capital efficiency, and asset liquidity, is likely to evolve through digital architecture. Rather than position himself in opposition to existing systems, he focused on how compliant, regulated alternatives could be thoughtfully designed within institutional frameworks. To date, Chad and his team have conducted structured analysis on more than 46 companies nationally across digital asset infrastructure, tokenization platforms, exchanges, custodians, and asset-backed finance models.

Chad approaches innovation as a discipline rather than an event. Over the past decade he has formally documented and catalogued hundreds of inventions and concepts across multiple industries. These are not speculative claims of execution, but structured examinations of how systems could evolve under different constraints. Through this process he has developed a strong capacity for pattern recognition: identifying structural inefficiencies, connecting adjacent strengths, and isolating leverage points where thoughtful redesign can unlock material improvement.

As Founder and CEO of KCS Capital, Chad now channels that discipline into the planning and development of 4orm Finance, an institution-grade digital asset infrastructure and national marketplace architecture designed to operate within regulated financial environments. Operating at the convergence of industrial assets, institutional finance, and digital infrastructure, his focus remains consistent: design systems that are stable, compliant, and built to last.

"The visionaries of today bold enough to take action will lead the future of tomorrow."
Chad R. Johnston

Co-Founder

Sam Mraiheen

Partnership Advisor

Sam Jr. Mraiheen is a Co-Founder of KCS Capital and advises the firm on institutional relations, supporting executive-level engagement across banking, regulatory, and capital markets institutions. His focus is to ensure that innovation is introduced within governance-aligned frameworks that institutions can responsibly evaluate and adopt.

Born in Kuwait and raised across the Middle East during a period of regional instability, Sam developed an early appreciation for resilience, capital preservation, and the importance of infrastructure that functions reliably under pressure. Those experiences shaped his long-term orientation toward disciplined system design and structured modernization.

Coming from a third-generation entrepreneurial family, Sam was exposed early to cross-border commerce and capital formation. During Dubai's rapid economic expansion in the early 2000s, his family built and scaled an automotive import and distribution enterprise. Observing both market volatility and accelerated growth cycles strengthened his understanding of asset turnover, operational discipline, and strategic positioning.

After relocating to Canada, Sam built his career within renewable energy infrastructure. As Vice President of Major Business Development and Executive Partner at Energy Economics Development Inc., he led utility-scale solar and battery storage initiatives, aligning engineering execution with structured capital deployment and multi-stakeholder coordination. His focus was translating decentralized infrastructure projects into commercially viable, institution-ready investment opportunities.

At KCS Capital, Sam functions as the firm's institutional bridge, connecting financial institutions, regulators, capital partners, and infrastructure operators with compliant digital asset initiatives. He focuses on governance alignment, counterparty selection, and structured engagement models that integrate programmable asset systems within existing regulatory environments.

Sam views the current evolution in financial markets not as disruption, but as a modernization cycle, one where digital infrastructure can enhance transparency, settlement efficiency, and capital access when implemented responsibly. His approach centres on institutional credibility, disciplined execution, and long-term resilience.

Advisor · Capital Markets

Bruce Fair

President, Mench Capital Corp.

Bruce was born in Saskatoon, Saskatchewan and has lived in Calgary, Vancouver, and Vernon. He is the President of Mench Capital Corp., founded in June 1999, a Canadian merchant banking firm providing corporate finance consulting and advisory services and access to private and public capital for established mid-market companies. Bruce is a seasoned merchant banker and capital markets executive with more than 25 years of experience structuring and originating private and public equity transactions across Canada.

Over the course of his career, Mench Capital Corp. has participated in or originated more than $500 million in private and public equity transactions, primarily across the Canadian resource sector in oil, gas, and mining, as well as structured and alternative investment products including private debt strategies and an innovative structure in the Canadian industrial carbon credits industry.

Bruce has held senior leadership roles including Vice President of Business Development at Qwest Bancorp Ltd., Executive Vice President and Director of Maple Leaf Funds, Regional Director at Next Edge Capital, Managing Director of Business Development at Harbourfront Wealth Management, and President of Cordilleran Resources Management Group Ltd. He has extensive experience developing tax-efficient investment vehicles, including flow-through limited partnerships that provide diversified exposure to Canadian junior exploration companies.

Bruce has served on numerous private and public company boards as an independent director, contributing governance oversight, strategic capital structuring, and institutional market insight. He was a founder of Sky Energy Partners, focused on solar and renewable energy from 2010 to 2014, and, along with Sol-Powered Energy Corp., funded and built the Casselman Arena rooftop solar project in Casselman, Ontario. He was formerly President and CEO of Cordillera Minerals Group Ltd. and President and CEO of both the Cordillera Minerals 2021 and 2023 Flow-Through Limited Partnerships.

Recently, Mench Capital Corp. provided financial advisory consulting for Ridge North America in 2025 and participated in securing $74 million in equity funding for the RNA Live Play Stay Development Limited Partnership, advancing a high-impact real estate and lifestyle investment initiative. Mench Capital also provided financial consulting services for CellCore Technologies Inc., Canada's first Indigenous nitrocellulose munitions facility, based in Saskatchewan. Bruce currently acts as Senior Vice President, Western Canada for Next Edge Capital, raising funds for critical and precious metals flow-through limited partnerships.

Bruce currently serves as an independent Director of Golden Sky Minerals and Thunderbird Minerals Corp. His prior board roles include Searchlight Resources Inc., Maple Leaf Energy Income Partnerships, Maple Leaf Flow-Through Limited Partnerships, Nation Wide Self Storage Trust I, Richfield Ventures Corp, Orsa Ventures, Cliffmont Resources, and Maple Leaf Resource Corp.

At KCS Capital, Bruce supports the development of institution-grade capital strategies as the firm builds regulated digital asset infrastructure and real-world asset tokenization platforms. As Capital Markets Advisor, he provides strategic guidance on capital formation, investor positioning, and institutional market access. Bruce holds an Honours degree in Political Science and English from the University of Saskatchewan.
